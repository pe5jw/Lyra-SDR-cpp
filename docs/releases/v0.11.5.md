# Lyra v0.11.5

**Hardware / scope:** Hermes Lite 2 / 2+ over HPSDR Protocol 1. RX, plus
CW / SSB / AM / DSB / SAM / FM and digital (TCI / VAC) transmit. **Windows
only** for now (Linux / macOS planned, not yet buildable).

A small polish release: setting up per-band power calibration is now
harder to get wrong.

## Ready-to-calibrate indicator (Settings → PA Gain)

Calibrating a band needs a **full-drive TUN in a voice mode** — but the two
easy trips (being in CW, or tuning below full drive) used to fail silently.
Now a live indicator sits right next to the **Calibrate this band** button and
tells you where you stand at a glance:

- **green `READY ✓`** — you're set (voice mode, full drive).
- **red `⚠ IN CW — CAN'T TUNE`** — the band is in CW, which makes no steady
  tune carrier. Mode is remembered per band, so it's easy to land on 15 / 10 m
  in CW without noticing — switch to **SSB / AM / FM** to calibrate.
- **amber `⚠ …% — NOT FULL`** — the drive TUN will use isn't 100 %.

It follows the **right** drive automatically: your **TX Drive** slider, or —
if you've set a separate tune drive in **Settings → TX** — the **Tune** slider
or **Fixed** tune drive, and it names which one to bump.

---

**Upgrade note:** nothing about your operating changes — this is purely a
clearer calibration setup. If your bands are already calibrated, they stay
calibrated.
