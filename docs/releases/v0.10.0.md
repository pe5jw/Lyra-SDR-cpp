# Lyra v0.10.0

**Hardware / scope:** Hermes Lite 2 / 2+ over HPSDR Protocol 1. RX, plus
CW / SSB / AM / DSB / SAM / FM and digital (TCI / VAC) transmit. **Windows
only** for now (Linux / macOS planned, not yet buildable).

The headline is the **voice keyer** — the phone-mode counterpart to the CW
macro bank. Record your standard messages once (a CQ call, a contest
exchange, a "stand by") and send them on the air from a labelled button or an
**F-key**. The same engine also **records what you hear** to a WAV file, and
lets you **review a clip locally** before you ever key up. This is the last
feature release before the two big arcs — a second receiver (RX2) and
PureSignal.

## Voice keyer (header → Options → Voice Keyer)

A floating panel, laid out like the CW console's macro chips.

- **Record a message** — pick **Mic** (your voice, for a keyer clip) or
  **RX** (the receiver audio you're listening to), press **REC**, press
  **Stop**. The clip is auto-named and appears as a labelled row.
- **Get-set countdown** — an optional **5 s / 10 s** timer counts down on the
  REC button (**⏱ 5… ⏱ 4…**) so you can get ready before capture starts;
  click again to cancel.
- **Two ways to play** — **▶ Review** plays a clip through your speakers
  **without transmitting** (audition it first); **▶ OTA** (or the clip's
  F-key in a voice mode) **transmits** it.
- **Manage clips** — rename, assign an **F-key**, set a per-clip **gain**
  trim, or **delete** (trash-can). **Import WAV** brings in a clip recorded
  elsewhere. Clips are saved as 48 kHz WAV in `%APPDATA%\Lyra\clips\`.
- **Gain three ways** — a global keyer level, a per-clip trim, and the input
  level while recording. The OTA level is **live while you transmit**, so you
  can ride it mid-message.

### On-air transmit is opt-in and PureSignal-safe

OTA transmit sits behind an amber **Enable TX** toggle, **off by default**.
Until you arm it, the keyer can only **Review** locally — so you can build and
audition your whole clip bank with zero risk of keying up. With it armed, a
clip is injected at the **mic input with MOX**, so it runs your **full TX DSP
chain** (EQ / speech / compression / ALC) and is **PureSignal-safe**, exactly
like your live voice. A **Bypass DSP** toggle sends the clip as-recorded
instead. The keyer keys with its own priority and **never overrides** a
manual, foot-switch, or VOX key.

## RX / TX recorder

The recorder shares the keyer engine, so grabbing audio is the same three
buttons: set the source to **RX** to capture what you hear (a weak signal, an
odd bit of traffic) or **Mic** to record a message, **REC**, **Stop**. Every
recording is a normal WAV file you can keep, share, or import back as a keyer
clip.

- **Maximum length** and the **record countdown** live in
  **Settings → Audio → "Voice keyer recording"** (default cap **5 minutes**).

## Also in this release

- **Trash-can delete** — the delete control in the Voice Keyer and the CW
  console is now a trash-can icon, matching the Tuner panel.
- **Amp "low-power" trap notice** — when the watts-cap parks well under your
  target because the drive is sitting at the PA floor (a coarse-step /
  untuned-fallback case), Lyra now shows a **CAP** indicator and a plain-
  language notice instead of leaving you guessing why the power is low.

---

**Upgrade note:** the voice keyer's on-air transmit is **off by default** —
open the panel, record and **Review** your clips freely, and only tick
**Enable TX** when you're ready to send them on the air. Nothing about your
existing operating changes until you do.
