# Lyra v0.12.0

**Hardware / scope:** Hermes Lite 2 / 2+ over HPSDR Protocol 1. RX, plus
CW / SSB / AM / DSB / SAM / FM and digital (TCI / VAC) transmit. **Windows
only** for now (Linux / macOS planned, not yet buildable).

A settings-safety and polish release. The headline is a full **Backup &
Restore** system, plus a layout **undo**, a crash-safe graphics fallback, a
more reliable space-bar PTT, and a tighter waterfall-ID that stays inside your
amateur bands.

## Backup & Restore (Settings → Backup & Restore)

A new tab that makes your whole Lyra configuration safe to keep, move, and
recover:

- **Export to a file** — write your entire config to a `.lyra` file (remembers
  your last folder). Share a setup, stash it before an experiment, or carry it
  to another PC.
- **Selective restore** — restoring shows a checklist of sections (Radio &
  connection, Audio & VAC, RX/DSP, TX chain, CW, Calibration & meters, Spots,
  Weather, Network, Memory/bands/tuner, Profiles, Display, Panel layout). Bring
  back **just the part you broke** and leave everything else alone. Panel layout
  is unticked by default so a restore doesn't rearrange your windows unless you
  ask.
- **Automatic dated snapshots** — Lyra quietly saves a snapshot every so many
  launches (both the cadence and how many to keep are adjustable on the tab).
  They live in a folder on disk — **Open folder** shows you where, and
  **Export selected…** copies one out to a USB stick or the cloud. Because
  they're files (not registry data), they **survive an uninstall/reinstall and
  even a settings wipe** — a fresh install can restore your old station straight
  from the snapshot folder.

Machine-specific keys — your radio's IP and your chosen graphics backend —
never travel in a backup, so a shared or restored profile carries only your
settings, not another PC's hardware identity.

## Layout undo

Dropped a dock panel in the wrong place while rearranging? **View → Layouts →
Undo layout change** steps the last one or two moves back to where they came
from — no manual re-drag, no full reset. (Reset-to-factory and the four named
layout slots are still there under the same menu.)

## Crash-safe graphics ("safe mode after crash")

Lyra chooses the best graphics backend for your GPU (Vulkan / Direct3D12 /
Direct3D11 / OpenGL — the picker lives at the bottom of **Settings → Visuals**).
If a launch ever dies *during* start-up — usually a flaky GPU driver — the
**next** launch now automatically drops to a safer backend (OpenGL, then
software) so you get back in without hand-editing the registry or an
environment variable. Once you're running, a one-time prompt lets you keep the
safe mode or go back to your chosen backend next time. If safe mode keeps
kicking in, update your GPU driver.

## Space-bar PTT that doesn't steal focus

If you use the space bar as PTT, it sometimes failed to key and instead popped
the Mode dropdown (or clicked a band button) when one of those had keyboard
focus. Lyra now previews the space bar app-wide — like the reference's key
preview — so it keys the radio reliably no matter which control is focused, and
still types a space normally when you're in a text field.

## Waterfall ID locked to amateur bands

The waterfall callsign ID is now **region-aware and ham-band-only**: it can't be
armed or fired outside an amateur allocation for your band-plan region. Spin
onto a broadcast or CB segment with it armed and it simply won't raster your
call out of band. (The existing 11m/CB lockout is part of this.)

---

**Upgrade note:** nothing about how you operate changes. Your existing settings,
profiles, and calibrations are untouched — this release just makes them safer to
keep and recover, and tightens a couple of rough edges.
