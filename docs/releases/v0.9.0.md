# Lyra v0.9.0

**Hardware / scope:** Hermes Lite 2 / 2+ over HPSDR Protocol 1. RX, plus
CW / SSB / AM / DSB / SAM / FM and digital (TCI / VAC) transmit. **Windows
only** for now (Linux / macOS planned, not yet buildable).

The headline is **VOX** — key up just by talking. Alongside it, the
amplifier watts-cap got a real accuracy overhaul (your on-screen watts now
match your external meter, and the cap holds rock-steady), the S-meter can
run a **different look on transmit than on receive**, and the 11m/CB band
got its **fun channel nicknames back on-screen**. Plus two CW fixes and a
tidier, safer Settings dialog.

## VOX — voice-operated transmit (Settings → VOX)

Key up just by talking — no PTT, no foot switch. VOX gets its **own Settings
tab** so it's easy to dial in:

- **Threshold** (mic level that opens TX), **open delay** (how quickly it
  keys), and **hang time** (how long it holds between words).
- **Anti-VOX** — suppresses tripping on your own receiver audio coming back
  through the mic, with its own level control.
- **Two live bar meters** — a **mic-level** bar (marker tracks your
  threshold) and an **anti-VOX** bar (RX audio) — so you set both by eye
  instead of guessing.
- Voice modes only (never on CW), **never overrides** a manual or
  foot-switch key, releases only its own key, and **turns off instantly**
  when you disable it.
- Off by default; PureSignal-safe (it only reads levels and makes the
  keying decision — it doesn't touch the TX audio).

The front-panel **VOX** button now toggles it directly (and the whole
MOX / Tune / VOX / ATT / Prot button cluster is unified in size, with
**TUN renamed "Tune"**).

## Amplifier watts-cap + power calibration (Settings → PA Gain)

The per-band **PA Gain** table and the **auto-tuning watts-output cap** got
an accuracy pass so the numbers you see are the numbers that are real:

- **One calibrated watts value everywhere.** The PWR meter, the watts cap,
  and your external watt-meter now all agree — calibrate a band once and the
  meter reads true. (A leftover global scale that was quietly multiplying on
  top of the per-band trim — making a 3.5 W cap read ~3.9 W — is gone; the
  per-band trim is the single calibration.)
- **The cap holds steady, just under your number.** The Hermes Lite's drive
  control is coarse (~16 hardware steps), so a cap often falls *between* two
  steps. Lyra now **parks on the highest step that stays under your cap**
  instead of hunting across the gap — so a band sits rock-steady a little
  under the cap (the safe side for your amp) rather than flickering over it.
  The "why it lands a little under" reasoning is spelled out right on the
  tab and in the User Guide.

## Meter — separate RX / TX style (Settings → Meter)

The signal meter can now use a **different visual style on transmit than on
receive**. Tick **Separate RX / TX meter style** and pick one style for RX
and another for TX — for example **Horizon Arc** while listening and
**Vertical Ladder** for a clean PWR / SWR read on transmit. It swaps
automatically the instant you key up and back on unkey (same as the meter's
source swap). The header **Arc | Bar | Ladder** buttons still work as a live
override.

## 11m / CB channel nicknames — back on-screen

The fun CB channel tags — **Super Bowl** (ch6), **Emergency** (ch9),
**Calling** (ch11), **Highway / truckers** (ch19), **SSB** (ch38/40) — now
draw **directly on the panadapter** under the channel number, so you see
them **even with tooltips turned off** (previously they only showed on
hover). Enable the 11m/CB band in Settings and tune to the 27 MHz range.

## CW fixes

- **High-speed keyer timing.** The internal keyer now schedules on
  microsecond resolution, removing the integer-rounding bias that skewed
  dit/dah and mark/space ratio above ~27–29 WPM. (For very high-speed CW a
  paddle or external keyer into the KEY jack is still the crispest route.)
- **CW console free-hand input.** The type-and-send field in the floating CW
  console could clip off the bottom of the dock — it's now pinned so it's
  always visible.

## Polish + safety

- **Panel "?" help** — each panel's **?** now deep-links to the right place:
  its Settings tab when it has one, or its section in the User Guide when it
  doesn't. CTUN and WF-ID header chips gained a right-click Help option.
- **Settings → TX** reflowed from two columns to **three** (Timing + Safety /
  Audio + Gain / Tune + Modes) so it isn't one long tall stack, and every
  spin box on the tab is a compact width.
- **Disclaimer** — a plain-language "use at your own risk / no warranty / no
  liability" note added to **Help → About Lyra** and the User Guide. It
  states clearly that the protection features (watts cap, SWR fold, TX
  timeout, etc.) are **aids, not guarantees** — always verify with a dummy
  load and your own instruments, and stay within your licence privileges.

---

**Upgrade note:** VOX and the separate RX/TX meter style are **off by
default** — nothing changes until you turn them on. If you use the watts
cap, re-key **TUN** on each band once after updating so the cap re-learns
with the new calibrated servo.
