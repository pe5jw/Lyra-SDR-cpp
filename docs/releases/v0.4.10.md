# Lyra v0.4.10

**Hardware / scope:** Hermes Lite 2 / 2+ over HPSDR Protocol 1. RX, plus
CW / SSB / AM / DSB / SAM / FM and digital (TCI / VAC) transmit.

This release fixes CTUN (Click-Tune) so it behaves like the reference, and
rolls in the rest of the CW decoder accuracy "A-series."

## Click-Tune (CTUN) now drags the frequency marker

With **CTUN** engaged the spectrum stays locked in place and you tune by
moving the **frequency marker** — exactly like the reference:

- **Drag anywhere in the panadapter body** and the orange frequency marker
  (and its filter box) **follows your cursor across the frozen spectrum** —
  drop it on a signal to tune it in.
- **Click outside the filter box** jumps the marker to the click; **click
  inside the box** does *not* yank the 0-beat to the cursor, so a stray
  click near your own signal won't knock you off frequency.
- **Drag the top edge** of the panadapter to pan the locked display while
  staying on the signal.

Previously the marker stayed pinned to the centre and the body-drag panned
the wrong way under CTUN. CTUN-off tuning is unchanged.

## CW decoder accuracy — the rest of the "A-series"

Building on the A-slice (nearest-code rescue, AFC centre-bias, per-character
confidence, crash-proof peak snap), this adds:

- **Sub-bin AFC** — 3-point parabolic interpolation refines the locked pitch
  below the coarse scan step, for tighter tracking of drifting/chirpy signals.
- **Hysteretic squelch gate** — once a signal opens the gate it stays open
  through a deeper QSB dip, so a fade mid-character no longer chops the letter.
- **Cleaner word spacing** — a single word gap can no longer emit a doubled
  space.
- **More punctuation** — `_` and `$` decode instead of `?`.

All of it is additive — a correct decode is never altered, and none of the
field-tuned timing constants changed.
